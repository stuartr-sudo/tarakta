Training Guide

Everything you need to understand how Tarakta works. Click any section to expand it.

01 System Overview ▶

Tarakta is an automated cryptocurrency trading bot that combines Smart Money Concepts (institutional trading strategies) with AI-powered decision making.

Core idea: The bot watches crypto markets around the clock, identifies trading opportunities that institutional traders would look for, then uses three AI agents to decide whether and when to trade.

The Pipeline

Scanner checks markets → Agent 1 analyses signal → Agent 2 decides entry timing → Trade executes → Agent 3 manages position

The system runs 24/7 and scans markets on a regular timer (configurable from the Settings page).

Paper Mode vs Live Mode

  • Paper mode (simulated): Uses real market data but no real money changes hands. Trades are tracked as if they were real, so you can evaluate performance without risk.
  • Live mode (real money): Real orders are placed on the exchange. Actual funds are at stake.

The mode banner at the top of every page always tells you which mode is active.

02 The Three AI Agents ▶

Tarakta uses three specialised AI agents, each with a distinct role. Think of them as a team of analysts working together.

Agent 1 — Signal Analyst

Answers the question: "Is this trade worth taking?"

Agent 1 looks at sweep quality, market structure, and overall context. It returns one of three verdicts:

  • SETUP_CONFIRMED: The signal looks good, proceed to entry timing.
  • WAIT_PULLBACK: The setup is valid but wait for a better entry price.
  • SKIP: Pass on this trade entirely.

Agent 2 — Entry Timer

Answers the question: "Is NOW the right time to enter?"

Agent 2 monitors price action on 5-minute candles. It only ever returns ENTER with the exact entry price, stop loss, take profit levels, and position size. If conditions are not right, it simply does not respond.

Agent 3 — Position Manager

Answers the question: "Should I adjust this open trade?"

Agent 3 monitors open trades and can do exactly two things:

  • Tighten the stop-loss: Move it closer to the current price to protect profits already gained.
  • Extend TP3: If momentum is strong, let the winning trade run further.

Agent 3 cannot close trades — that is handled automatically by the exchange when price hits the stop-loss or take-profit levels.

Model Selection

Each agent uses an OpenAI GPT model. You can choose between faster, cheaper models (nano, mini) or smarter, more expensive ones (full GPT-5.3, GPT-5.4) from the Settings page. Each agent can also be individually enabled or disabled.

03 Signal Scanning & Smart Money Concepts ▶

Scanning means checking all configured markets every few minutes for potential trade setups. The scanner looks for patterns that institutional ("smart money") traders typically create.

Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

When price moves so fast it leaves an "imbalance" — a gap in the price action. Price often returns to fill these gaps, making them predictable entry points.

Order Blocks (OBs)

Areas where large institutional traders previously bought or sold heavily. These zones act as strong support (price bounces up) or resistance (price bounces down).

Liquidity Sweeps

When price briefly spikes above or below a key level to trigger stop-losses, then reverses. This is how institutions collect liquidity before making a real move. A sweep often signals a genuine reversal is about to start.

Market Structure

The pattern of price movement over time:

  • Uptrend: A pattern of higher highs and higher lows.
  • Downtrend: A pattern of lower highs and lower lows.

The bot trades in the direction of the current structure, not against it.

Confluence Scoring

Each signal gets a score based on how many indicators agree. If an FVG, order block, and liquidity sweep all point the same direction, that is high confluence and a higher confidence trade. The minimum score needed to trade is configurable (Entry Threshold in Settings).
04 Risk Management & Safety ▶

Risk management is the most important part of any trading system. These controls exist to protect your capital even when individual trades go wrong.

Max Risk Per Trade

Limits how much of your balance can be lost on any single trade. For example, if set to 2%, a $10,000 balance means no single trade can lose more than $200.

Circuit Breaker

Your emergency brake. Automatically stops ALL trading if total losses exceed a threshold (e.g., 15% of balance). This prevents catastrophic losses during unusual market conditions. Trading resumes only after a manual review.

Daily Drawdown Limit

The maximum loss allowed in a single day before trading pauses automatically. If the bot loses too much in one day, it stops and waits until the next day.

Max Concurrent Positions

How many trades can be open at the same time. This prevents the bot from being overexposed to the market.

Max Exposure

The total portfolio exposure limit across all open positions combined.

Cooldown

After a string of losses, the bot waits a configurable amount of time before trading again. This prevents "tilt" — chasing losses with increasingly risky trades.

All of these settings are configurable from the Settings page. It is strongly recommended not to change risk settings without understanding their impact.
05 Position Management & Trade Lifecycle ▶

Trade Flow

Signal found → Agent 1 approves → Queued for Agent 2 → Agent 2 says ENTER → Order placed

Take-Profit Levels

Each trade has three take-profit targets, which close portions of the position as price moves in your favour:

  • TP1: Quick profit — closes a portion of the position early to lock in some gains.
  • TP2: Larger move — closes another portion at a higher profit target.
  • TP3: Full target — the remaining position closes here for maximum profit.

Stop-Loss (SL)

The price level where the trade closes at a loss to prevent further damage. Every trade has a stop-loss — this is non-negotiable. It is your maximum downside on that trade.

How Agent 3 Manages Trades

While a trade is open, Agent 3 can:

  • Tighten SL: If the trade is in profit, move the stop-loss closer to the entry price or into profit. This way, even if the trade reverses, you keep some of the gains.
  • Extend TP3: If momentum is strong, push the final target further to let the winner run.

How Trades Close

  • Hits the stop-loss (loss)
  • Hits all take-profit levels (full profit)
  • Maximum hold time is exceeded (time-based exit)
06 Dashboard Guide ▶

The navigation bar at the top links to every section. Here is what each page shows:

Dashboard (/)

Live overview of the system: current balance, profit and loss, any open positions, and whether the engine is running.

Trades (/trades)

Complete history of every trade the bot has taken. Shows entry and exit prices, profit or loss, duration, and which agents were involved.

Signals (/signals)

Every signal the scanner detected, whether it was actually traded or not. Useful for reviewing what the bot saw versus what it acted on.

Analytics (/analytics)

Performance charts including win rate over time, cumulative profit and loss, and reward-to-risk distribution.

Usage (/usage)

API costs for running the AI agents. Track how much is being spent per agent, per day. Useful for cost management.

Chart (/chart)

TradingView chart for manual analysis of any trading pair. Good for visually verifying what the bot is seeing.

Settings (/settings)

Configure everything: leverage, risk parameters, agent models, trading mode, and more.

07 Paper vs Live Trading ▶

Paper Trading

Uses real market data but all trades are simulated. No real money is at risk. Perfect for testing strategies, learning the system, and evaluating performance before committing real funds.

Live Trading

Real orders are placed on the exchange. Real money is used. Every profit is real, and every loss is real.

How to Switch

Go to the Settings page and change the Trading Mode toggle. This is an admin-only action.

Before Going Live

A recommended checklist:

  • Look for consistent paper trading profits over at least several weeks.
  • Understand every risk setting and what it controls.
  • Start with small position sizes — you can always increase later.
  • Monitor the dashboard daily, especially in the first week.
08 Settings & Configuration ▶

All settings are on the Settings page and take effect immediately — no restart needed.

Leverage

How much borrowing power you are using. For example, 10x means $100 of your balance controls $1,000 worth of a position. Higher leverage = more potential profit AND more potential loss.

Margin Per Trade

What percentage of your balance to use per trade. A smaller margin means smaller positions and lower risk per trade.

Entry Threshold

The minimum confluence score a signal needs before it is considered for trading. A higher threshold means fewer trades but higher quality setups.

Agent Models

Choose the AI model each agent uses:

  • Faster/cheaper models (nano, mini): Lower cost per decision, slightly less nuanced analysis.
  • Smarter/more expensive models (full GPT-5.3, GPT-5.4): Better analysis but higher API costs.

Agent Toggles

You can enable or disable each agent individually. Disabling an agent means its step in the pipeline is skipped.

09 Understanding the Analytics ▶

Key Metrics

Win Rate: Percentage of trades that were profitable. Above 50% is good, above 60% is excellent.
P&L (Profit and Loss): Your net result in dollars. The cumulative P&L chart shows the overall performance trend over time.
R:R Ratio (Reward-to-Risk): If you risk $10 and gain $30, that is 3:1 R:R. An average above 2:1 across all trades is the target.

Spotting Problems

  • Declining win rate over time — the strategy may need adjustment.
  • Large individual losses — risk settings may be too aggressive.
  • Many consecutive losses — the market conditions may not suit the strategy.

What Success Looks Like

  • A steady upward P&L curve over weeks and months.
  • Consistent R:R above 2:1 on average.
  • Win rate holding above 50%.
10 Glossary & Key Terms ▶
Circuit Breaker An automatic safety switch that halts all trading when total losses exceed a set threshold. Prevents catastrophic drawdowns.
Confluence When multiple technical indicators agree on the same trade direction. More confluence means higher confidence in the trade.
Drawdown The decline from a peak balance to a trough. A 10% drawdown on a $10,000 account means the balance dropped to $9,000.
Entry Threshold The minimum confluence score a signal must reach before it is considered for trading. Higher threshold = fewer, higher-quality trades.
Fair Value Gap (FVG) A price imbalance created when price moves too fast, leaving a gap. Price tends to return to fill these gaps.
Leverage Borrowing power that amplifies your position size. 10x leverage means $100 controls a $1,000 position. Amplifies both gains and losses equally.
Liquidity Sweep When price briefly spikes past a key level to trigger stop-losses, then reverses. Often signals the start of a genuine price move.
Margin The amount of your own capital used to open a leveraged position. Your actual money at risk in a trade.
Market Structure The pattern of highs and lows that defines a trend. Higher highs/higher lows = uptrend. Lower highs/lower lows = downtrend.
Order Block A price zone where institutional traders previously placed large orders. Acts as strong support or resistance.
Paper Trading Simulated trading using real market data but no real money. Used for testing and evaluation.
P&L Profit and Loss. The net financial result of trading activity, measured in dollars.
R:R Ratio Reward-to-Risk ratio. Compares potential profit to potential loss. A 3:1 R:R means you stand to gain 3x what you are risking.
SL (Stop Loss) A pre-set price level where a trade automatically closes to limit losses. Every trade must have one.
Smart Money Concepts (SMC) A trading methodology based on how institutional traders (banks, hedge funds) operate. Focuses on order blocks, liquidity sweeps, and fair value gaps.
TP (Take Profit) A pre-set price level where a trade automatically closes to lock in profit. Tarakta uses three levels: TP1, TP2, and TP3.
